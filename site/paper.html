Whitepaper

Design rationale and formal specification for the Pyana authorization system. Covers the core insight (capability attenuation as IVC), the STARK proof construction, and the federation consensus model.

Abstract

We present Pyana, a distributed authorization system that combines object-capability semantics with zero-knowledge proofs to enable AI agents to prove authorization across organizational boundaries without revealing their delegation chain. The core insight is that capability attenuation — the monotonic narrowing of permissions through delegation — is structurally identical to incrementally verifiable computation, allowing ZK presentation to emerge naturally from the authorization model rather than being bolted on post hoc.

The system achieves sub-second proof generation (~24 KiB STARK proofs in 300–560ms), sub-millisecond verification, offline operation without blockchain liveness, and post-quantum security for external interfaces through exclusively hash-based verification.
