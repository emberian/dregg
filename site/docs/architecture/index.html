Architecture

Pyana is a sovereign capability runtime. Agents own their state as cells on their own machines. The federation is a notary -- used for ordering, double-spend prevention, discovery, and proving to strangers. Between known parties, it is just signed proofs exchanged directly. The system spans ~125k lines of Rust across 22+ crates.

Three Interaction Modes

  • Peer-to-peer -- Sovereign cells interact directly. No federation contact. Agents exchange signed state transitions with STARK proofs of validity. The recipient verifies locally. Used for routine interactions between known parties.
  • Federation-mediated -- When ordering, atomicity, or nullifier tracking is needed. The federation verifies proofs and provides ordering guarantees, but never sees cell contents -- only 32-byte commitments.
  • Hosted (legacy/convenience) -- The federation maintains full state for a cell. Used for shared mutable objects (orderbooks, registries) or agents that prefer convenience over sovereignty.

What the Federation Does NOT Do

  • Federation does NOT see your cell state (sovereign mode -- only a 32-byte commitment).
  • Federation does NOT see transfer amounts (Pedersen committed conservation).
  • Federation does NOT know who you are sending to (stealth addresses).
  • Federation does NOT execute your transitions (you prove locally, it verifies).

What the Federation DOES Provide

  • Ordering -- when two cells interact with the same third party and sequence matters.
  • Nullifier tracking -- double-spend prevention for the shared note tree.
  • Discovery -- gossip network for finding parties you do not already know.
  • Root anchoring -- periodic attested checkpoints so you can prove state validity to strangers.

Design Principles

  • Capability attenuation IS incrementally verifiable computation. Each delegation is a fold over a committed fact set. The STARK proves a valid chain.
  • Offline-first verification. A STARK proof + attested root is complete. No federation liveness required.
  • Agents own their state. Cells exist on the agent's machine. The federation tracks commitments (32 bytes per cell), not full state.
  • Federation as notary. You use the federation when you need to prove something to strangers or resolve disputes. Not for every operation.
  • Progressive privacy. Three modes (trusted, selective, private) let developers choose the right tradeoff per interaction.
  • Post-quantum security. STARK proofs are hash-based (Poseidon2/BLAKE3). No elliptic curve assumptions on the verification path.
